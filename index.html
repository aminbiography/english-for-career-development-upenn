Course Overview

This page is designed as a concise presentation note for GitHub or portfolio use. It turns the course into a visual study summary with clear unit goals, practical skills, and job-application workflow.

Five-Unit Structure

The course progresses through five major stages of career development, from understanding the job market to handling interviews.

Language + Career Skills

Each unit combines English practice with practical career tasks such as reading job ads, writing documents, networking, and answering interview questions.

Portfolio Friendly

This format works well as a static GitHub Pages project, a screenshot-based submission, or a personal learning archive.

Career Development Path

1. Enter the Job Market

Identify skills, interests, and job-search steps.

2. Build a Résumé

Organize qualifications, keywords, and action verbs.

3. Write a Cover Letter

Present personal style, qualifications, and interview request.

4. Network Professionally

Use small talk, professional relationships, and elevator speech.

5. Interview for a Job

Prepare examples, ask questions, and make a professional impression.

Unit 1

Entering the Job Market

Focuses on the U.S. job-search process, professional interests, job-market awareness, job-search vocabulary, and compare/contrast language structures.

Main Skills

  • Identify job-search steps
  • Understand career interests and skills
  • Read job advertisements

Language Focus

  • Simple present
  • Present progressive
  • Compare and contrast phrases
Unit 2

Résumés

Explains the purpose of a résumé, the structure of an American-style résumé, and the use of action verbs and keywords to communicate qualifications clearly.

Main Skills

  • Recognize key résumé components
  • Produce a one-page résumé
  • Match qualifications to a job ad

Language Focus

  • Action verbs
  • Key words
  • Clear achievement statements
Unit 3

Writing a Cover Letter

Introduces formal job-application writing, including heading, greeting, introduction, body paragraphs, and closing. It also emphasizes adapting each letter to the job.

Main Skills

  • Understand cover-letter structure
  • Connect skills to job description
  • Formally request an interview

Language Focus

  • Past tense
  • Present perfect
  • Modal verbs for politeness
Unit 4

Networking Skills

Covers networking as relationship building, the hidden job market, professional small talk, and the elevator speech as a concise professional introduction.

Main Skills

  • Build professional relationships
  • Prepare an elevator speech
  • Use networking for job discovery

Language Focus

  • Stress and rhythm
  • Intonation
  • Clear spoken introduction
Unit 5

Interviewing for a Job

Focuses on interview questions, interviewer and interviewee roles, clarification strategies, short examples from past experience, and professional presentation during interviews.

Main Skills

  • Answer interview questions clearly
  • Prepare examples from past work or study
  • Ask relevant questions to the interviewer

Language Focus

  • Question types
  • Clarification and repetition
  • Brief, specific answers

Course Reflection

This course enhanced my understanding of the complete job application process, including both new opportunities and career transitions, and strengthened my ability to communicate professionally in English. I developed practical, up-to-date skills in résumé writing, cover letter preparation, networking, and interview communication. The integration of language development with career strategy made the course highly relevant to real-world professional environments. It also improved my confidence in presenting my skills and experiences clearly and effectively.

This project is developed as part of personal study based on academically certified coursework.
